01 / The Problem

Will this cohort move the model — before you pay for the run?

Frontier labs buy expensive post-training data and burn full RL runs to find out whether it helps. The economic question is upstream of training: which cohorts earn their compute?

The buyer's dilemma

The answer arrives after the bill

Today, a cohort's value is revealed only after a complete GRPO run — accuracy-after minus accuracy-before on a fixed benchmark. Procurement, budget allocation, and data-QA decisions are all made blind, then validated by the most expensive possible measurement.

A reliable pre-purchase, pre-training score re-prices the entire post-training data market: allocate RL compute by predicted lift-per-dollar, and kill bad cohorts before they cost a run.

Where on the frontier?

The cost ↔ predictive-power tradeoff

Cheap proxies (token length, diversity) are nearly free but weak. Strong predictors (gradient influence, datamodels, a small RL run) are accurate but cost 100×–1000× more. The open question: can a signal live in the top-left — cheap and predictive?

LAD targets exactly that corner of the Pareto plane, and the study places it there empirically.

02 / The Insight

Lift is bounded by the advantage signal a cohort carries

GRPO's per-task gradient is driven by the spread of rewards within a group. For a binary verifier that spread is exactly p(1−p) — zero when the model always or never solves a task, maximal in the learnable band. LAD turns that theorem into a dataset score.

Advantage energy

Within-group reward variance for a binary verifier. The KL between the base policy and the RL-optimal policy is lower-bounded by this Bernoulli variance — it is the leading term of a proven bound on policy improvement.

(1 − p̂)γ

Headroom asymmetry

Variance is symmetric about 0.5, but lift is not — a cohort that mostly fails has more room to convert fail→pass into benchmark accuracy. The exponent γ tilts the peak toward harder-but-learnable tasks.

(Vendi / |C|)β

Effective diversity

Lift is bounded by distinct skills, not summed energy. The Vendi score — the effective number of unique tasks — divides out redundancy so near-duplicate cohorts cannot fake learnability.

03 / The Metric

LAD, in one line

Three justified factors: is there signal to learn from, is it pointed where lift lives, and is it non-redundant. Computed from k=32 base-model rollouts per task — no training, no gradients.

04 / Experimental Design

The cohort is the unit of measurement

Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct on GSM8K. 19 cohort families that vary one data property at a time — including families built to break naive metrics. Each runs identical GRPO (70 steps, 2 seeds); LAD is evaluated leave-one-cohort-out.

19 cohort families · 256 prompts each · one variable at a time
Protocol
STEP 01

Score once, up front

32 base-model rollouts per task across every cohort — computed once and reused for every metric in the family.

STEP 02

Train identical GRPO

Same model, budget, config, eval set, verifier per cohort. Cohort identity is the only variable; 2 seeds averaged.

STEP 03

Measure lift

Δacc = accuracy-after − accuracy-before on a fixed 500-question held-out GSM8K test set.

STEP 04

LOCO evaluation

Fit ŷ = a + b·LAD on training cohorts; report Spearman ρ on the left-out cohort. Honesty over in-sample fit.

08 / Conclusion

A pre-flight check for the post-training data economy

What the study shows

The four claims hold

LAD correlates with the realized GRPO signal (mechanistic), predicts held-out lift at ρ = 0.89 (predictive), beats every cheap baseline and matches expensive ones at a fraction of the cost (incremental), and selecting by LAD causes the largest lift at equal compute (causal).

The mechanism — advantage energy × headroom × effective diversity — is derived from the GRPO objective, so each factor carries a reason and a demonstrated failure mode it corrects.

Limitations
  • Validated on Qwen2.5-1.5B + GSM8K with a binary verifier; broader models and graded rewards extend the same derivation.
  • γ and β are fit on training cohorts and held fixed under LOCO; nested selection guards against overfitting at small n.
  • Lift is measured as pass@1 at a fixed sampling budget — the quantity a buyer ships — not a pass@k capability claim.
  • Adversarial broken-verifier cohorts remain the hardest case; the diversity and correctness terms down-weight but do not erase them.
