01 / mechanism

Lift comes from within-group reward spread — a theorem, not a hunch

GRPO scores each rollout by its group-relative advantage Ai = (ri − meanj rj) / (stdj rj + ε). For a binary verifier the within-group spread is exactly p(1−p) — zero when every rollout passes (already solved, no headroom) or every rollout fails (nothing to reinforce), maximal at the middle.

DKL( πinit ‖ π* )  ≥  p(T)·(1 − p(T)) / (2β²)     # Bae et al., EACL 2026, Prop 3.1 — maximized at p = ½

The expected policy improvement is lower-bounded by the Bernoulli variance of the pass-rate. DAPO, RL-ZVP and difficulty-aware staged RL all confirm the converse — zero-variance prompts teach nothing. They use this quantity online, to filter batches mid-run. LAD re-purposes the same provably-correct quantity, computed once from cheap base-model rollouts, as a pre-purchase value score — a different decision (procurement, not scheduling) for a different customer (a data marketplace, not the trainer loop).

correction · headroom

Lift is asymmetric

A cohort at p=0.3 has more room to rise (fail→pass) than one at p=0.7 with equal variance. (1−p̂)γ shifts the peak below ½.

γ=1 → peak at p ≈ 0.33
correction · redundancy

Distinct skills, not copies

256 paraphrases at p=0.5 have huge summed energy but teach one thing. The Vendi fraction is the effective number of distinct tasks.

(Vendi / |C|) ∈ (0, 1]
03 / held-out validation

Leave-one-cohort-out: does the cheap metric predict the expensive oracle?

We run one identical GRPO per size-matched cohort (cohort = the only variable), measure lift = accafter − accbefore on a fixed GSM8K test set, then hold each cohort out and predict its lift from the others. We lead with held-out Spearman ρ — honest, robust with few cohorts.
